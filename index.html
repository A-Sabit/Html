Ronaldinho

Ronaldo de Assis Moreira (born 21 March 1980), commonly known as Ronaldinho Gaúcho (Brazilian Portuguese: [ʁonawˈdʒĩɲu gaˈuʃu]) or simply Ronaldinho,[note 1] is a Brazilian former professional footballer and ambassador for Barcelona.[4] He played mostly as an attacking midfielder, but was also deployed as a forward or a winger. He played the bulk of his career at European clubs Paris Saint-Germain, Barcelona and A.C. Milan as well as playing for the Brazilian national team. Often considered one of the best players of his generation and regarded by many as one of the greatest of all time,[note 2] Ronaldinho won two FIFA World Player of the Year awards and a Ballon d'Or. He was renowned for his technical skills and creativity; due to his agility, pace and dribbling ability, as well as his use of tricks, feints, overhead kicks, no-look passes and accuracy from free-kicks.

Progress

  • 1997 - World Champion (under 17 years old).
  • 1999 - Winner of the America's Cup.
  • 1999 - Top scorer of the Rio Grande do Sul State Championship.
  • 1999 - The best scorer of the Confederations Cup.
  • 1999 - The best player in the Confederations Cup.
  • 2000 - Winner of the Silver Ball of Brazil.
  • 2001 - Winner of the Intertoto Cup.
  • 2002 - World Champion.
  • 2004 - The best foreign footballer of the championship of Spain
  • 2004/05, 2005/06 - Champion of Spain (2).
  • 2005 - Winner of the Confederations Cup.
  • 2005 - UEFA's best forward.
  • 2005 - The best player in the world according to Onze.
  • 2005, 2006 - Winner of the Spanish Super Cup (2).
  • 2005/06 - Winner of the Champions League.
  • 2008 - Bronze medalist of the Olympic Games.
  • 2008/09, 2009/10 - Bronze medalist of the Italian Championship (2).
  • 2009/10 - The best assistant to the championship of Italy.
